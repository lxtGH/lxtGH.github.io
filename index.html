About Me [GitHub] [Google Scholar] [CV] [知乎]

I am a PhD student from the School of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences, Peking University since 2017. I started my PhD study at Peking University after I finished my bachelor degree for Beijing University of Post and Telecommunication. My advisor is Prof. Yunhai Tong. In 2018-2019, I was a research intern mentored by Dr.Kuiyuan Yang at DeepMotion Perception Group where I started my first research. Now, I am a research intern at SenseTime mentored by Dr.Guangliang Cheng and Dr.Jianping Shi.

My recent works are mainly on segmentation in computer vision including semantic segmentation, instance segmentation, video instance segmentation. I also work closely with Dr.Li Zhang, Xia Li, Ansheng You,Zhen Zhu, Houlong Zhao.

I am looking for post doc research position or research position in industries.

Selected Publications

Semantic Flow for Fast and Accurate Scene Parsing
Xiangtai Li*, Ansheng You*, Zhen Zhu, Houlong Zhao, Maoke Yang, Kuiyuan Yang, Yunhai Tong
in ECCV, 2020, oral
[Paper] [Code]
We propose a flow based network for fast and accurate semantic segmentation. It is the first work that achieves 80 mIoU on Cityscapes while runing at real time. We opensource the code and model in the framework of TorchCV.
Improving Semantic Segmentation via Decoupled Body and Edge Supervision
Xiangtai Li*, Xia Li, Li Zhang, Guangliang Cheng, Jianping Shi,Zhouchen Lin, Yunhai Tong
in ECCV, 2020,
[Paper] [Code]
We propose a novel framework by decoupling the segmentation in body and edge part and optimize the both parts simultaneously. We achieve the top performance on four road driving datasets including Cityscapes, Camvid, BDD, Kitty.
Gated Fully Fusion for Semantic Segmentation
Xiangtai Li*, Houlong Zhao, Lei Han, Yunhai Tong, Shaohua Tan, Kuiyuan Yang
in AAAI, 2020, oral
[Paper]
We propose a new architecture, named Gated Fully Fusion (GFF), to selectively fuse features from multiple levels using gates in a fully connected way. We achieve the state of the art results on four challenging scene parsing datasets including Cityscapes, Pascal Context, COCO-stuff and ADE20K.
Global Aggregation then Local Distribution in Fully Convolutional Networks
Xiangtai Li*, Li Zhang, Ansheng You, Kuiyuan Yang, Yunhai Tong
in BMVC, 2019
[Paper] [Code]
We propose Global Aggregation then Local Distribution (GALD) scheme to distribute global information to each position adaptively according to the local information around the position. GALD net achieves top performance on Cityscapes dataset.
